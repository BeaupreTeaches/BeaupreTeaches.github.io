Teaching Profile

I have had the privilege of working with students who struggled with mathematics, and I was able to support them throughout their learning journey. Teachers who demonstrate unwavering dedication and a willingness to make sacrifices for their students often become the ones students truly cherish (Duncan-Andrade, 2009). One student, in particular, embodied this idea. When I began my student teaching placement, this student was failing. However, through consistent support and working closely together every day during their lunch break, we were able to significantly improve their grades. What started as a collaborative effort between us became a shared journey of dedication and perseverance, having both of us committed to reaching success. On a feedback evaluation, this student expressed their gratitude for the extra help they received, suggesting that I continue to provide additional supports to others. While my time with this student was brief, the experience had a lasting impact on both their academic and personal growth. This experience encapsulates my vision of what a teacher should strive to be—someone who makes a meaningful difference, no matter how small, by showing dedication, patience, and genuine care for students’ development.

1. Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy is grounded in the belief that every student has the ability to succeed when nurtured in a supportive, equitable, and intellectually stimulating environment. I strive to foster curiosity, critical thinking, and a growth mindset in all learners. I view my role not just as an instructor, but as a facilitator of meaningful learning experiences that are culturally relevant and personally empowering.

2. Classroom Management Style

I believe in building a classroom culture based on mutual respect, clear expectations, and restorative practices. I emphasize proactive classroom management by creating structured routines and establishing trust with students. I encourage student voice and provide opportunities for self-regulation, reflection, and collaborative problem-solving to maintain a positive learning environment.

3. Instructional Strategies

I use a variety of instructional strategies to reach all learners, particularly in mathematics. These include the use of manipulatives and visual representations to make abstract concepts tangible, integrating educational technology for interactive engagement, and applying real-world problems to foster relevance. I differentiate instruction based on student needs and provide opportunities for peer collaboration, inquiry-based learning, and hands-on exploration.

4. Assessment Philosophy

Assessment in my classroom is both a tool for learning and a reflection of growth. I incorporate a balance of formative and summative assessments to guide instruction and provide timely feedback. I believe in transparent grading practices and in using assessments to help students track their own progress. I also encourage self-assessment and reflection to promote metacognitive skills and learner autonomy.

5. Commitment to Equity and Inclusion

I am deeply committed to creating an inclusive classroom where all students feel valued, heard, and respected. I work to incorporate diverse voices and perspectives into my curriculum and practice. Whether it’s supporting English Language Learners, students with IEPs, or those from marginalized backgrounds, I tailor my teaching to meet their individual strengths and needs. I continuously reflect on my own biases and adapt my teaching to serve every student equitably.

6. Professional Development and Growth

As an educator, I am dedicated to lifelong learning. I regularly participate in professional development workshops, attend educational conferences, and read current research in pedagogy and mathematics education. I actively seek out mentorship and peer collaboration to refine my teaching practices and stay current with evidence-based strategies. My goal is to always be evolving in ways that better serve my students.

7. Collaboration and School Involvement

I believe that strong collaboration among educators, families, and communities enhances student success. I work closely with colleagues to align instruction, co-create interdisciplinary lessons, and contribute to team-based planning. I also engage with parents and caregivers through regular communication and involvement in school events. I am eager to contribute to the wider school culture through committee participation and student support initiatives.

8. Vision Statement

My ultimate goal as a teacher is to inspire students to believe in themselves, to persevere through challenges, and to discover joy in learning. I aim to cultivate not only strong mathematicians but also thoughtful, curious, and compassionate individuals. Through dedication, adaptability, and reflective practice, I strive to be an educator who leaves a lasting, positive impact on the lives of my students.
